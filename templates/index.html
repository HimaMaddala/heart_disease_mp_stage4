Input Information
  • Age: Enter your age in years (e.g., 25-100).
  • Sex: Select "Male" or "Female."
  • Chest Pain Type:
    • 0: Typical Angina
    • 1: Atypical Angina
    • 2: Non-anginal Pain
    • 3: Asymptomatic
  • Resting Blood Pressure: Normal range is 90-180 mmHg.
  • Cholesterol: Enter serum cholesterol in mg/dL (normal: <200 mg/dL).
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: High if >120 mg/dL (1 = High, 0 = Normal).
  • Rest ECG:
    • 0: Normal
    • 1: ST-T wave abnormality
    • 2: Left ventricular hypertrophy
  • Max Heart Rate: Enter the maximum heart rate achieved (normal: 60-220 bpm).
  • Exercise Induced Angina: 1 = Yes, 0 = No.
  • Oldpeak: ST depression relative to rest, e.g., 0.0-6.0.
  • Slope:
    • 0: Upsloping
    • 1: Flat
    • 2: Downsloping
  • CA: Number of major vessels (0-3) seen via fluoroscopy.
  • Thal:
    • 0: Normal
    • 1: Fixed defect
    • 2: Reversible defect

Precaution

We recommend consulting a certified cardiologist immediately. A cardiologist can help diagnose the severity of your condition and suggest the best course of action.

  • Schedule a comprehensive heart check-up.
  • Follow prescribed medications and therapies.
  • Avoid self-medication or delay in treatment.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.
  • Exercise regularly (30 minutes of moderate exercise daily).
  • Manage stress through meditation or yoga.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Get enough sleep (7-8 hours per night).
  • Monitor and control your blood pressure and cholesterol levels.
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day.
